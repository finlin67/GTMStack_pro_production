The traditional MQL is dead. Not because lead generation has failed, but because our interpretation of "intent" has been stuck in 2018. Demographic data—company size, job title, and industry—is no longer a predictor of success; it's merely a baseline for entry.

Module 01

Why the Old Approach Falls Short

Static scoring models treat a lead who visited your pricing page six months ago the same as a lead who visited it this morning. In a hyper-competitive GTM environment, this latency is fatal. Most CRM-based scoring relies on additive math that never accounts for the entropy of interest.

Module 02

Implementing the Stack Logic

To transition to kinetic scoring, you must decouple your data ingestion from your CRM's native calculation engine. We recommend a headless RevOps approach using a transformation layer (like dbt) to compute "Kinetic Scores" before pushing them back to Salesforce or HubSpot.

"Data without velocity is just inventory. Kinetic scoring turns your database into a high-frequency trading floor for sales opportunities."
kinetic_score.sql 
WITH signals AS (
  SELECT
    user_id,
    SUM(weight * EXP(-0.1 * DATEDIFF(day, occurred_at, CURRENT_DATE))) as decay_score
  FROM raw_events
  GROUP BY 1
)
UPDATE crm_leads
SET kinetic_intent_score = signals.decay_score
FROM signals
WHERE crm_leads.id = signals.user_id;

The above logic ensures that older signals naturally diminish in value. A whitepaper download from 3 months ago is worth 0.04 points, while a pricing page view from 2 hours ago is worth a full 10.0 points.
