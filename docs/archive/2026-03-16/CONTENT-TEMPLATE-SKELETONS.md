## GTMStack.pro — Content Template Skeletons

This document defines the **section skeletons** for each major page type so you can structure written content consistently.

Use the A/B/C labels as **ordered sections**, not as headings you show on the site. You can adjust wording inside each section, but keep the sequence so templates and layouts stay predictable.

---

## 1. Home

- **A. Hero & Promise**
  - Who you are and the primary value proposition.
  - 1–2 supporting sentences that clarify the outcome.
  - 1 primary CTA (e.g. "Work with us" / "View expertise") and 1 secondary CTA.

- **B. Who We Help (Industries Snapshot)**
  - Short intro line about the types of companies you support.
  - 4–8 key industries listed or grouped.
  - Optional link to full `Industries` hub.

- **C. How We Help (Expertise Snapshot)**
  - 1–2 sentence overview of your approach to GTM.
  - List or grid of the main expertise pillars (Content & Engagement, Demand & Growth, etc.).
  - Link to full `Expertise` hub.

- **D. Proof & Case Studies**
  - Short introduction framing why proof matters.
  - 3–6 highlighted case studies (titles, a 1-line outcome, key metric if available).
  - Link to `Case Studies` hub.

- **E. Featured Assets (Blog / Animations)**
  - 1–2 sentence explanation of why these resources exist (education, inspiration, credibility).
  - 2–4 featured blog posts or animations with short blurbs.
  - Link to `Blog` and/or `Animation Gallery`.

- **F. About Snapshot**
  - Short founder / team paragraph (who you are, your background).
  - 3–5 credibility signals (years of experience, notable logos, scale of work).
  - Link to full `About` page.

- **G. CTA / Next Step**
  - Strong summary sentence of value.
  - 1 primary CTA (e.g. "Book a working session").
  - Optional secondary CTA (e.g. "View case studies", "Browse expertise").

---

## 2. About

- **A. Overview & Mission**
  - 1–2 paragraph summary of GTMStack.pro.
  - Clear mission or philosophy statement.

- **B. Story / Background**
  - Founding story and context (what problem you saw in GTM).
  - How your experience led to this specific approach.

- **C. Approach & Principles**
  - 3–5 core principles (e.g. "evidence-driven GTM", "systems before tactics").
  - Brief explanation under each principle.

- **D. Experience & Credibility**
  - Snapshot of previous roles, organizations, or types of projects.
  - Key metrics or outcomes if available.

- **E. How We Work Together**
  - Typical engagement formats (e.g. audits, ongoing advisory, projects).
  - What clients can expect in the first 30–90 days.

- **F. Personal / Human Element**
  - Short humanizing paragraph (style, how you like to collaborate).
  - Optional small personal details that make you relatable.

- **G. CTA**
  - Invite to start a conversation.
  - Clear CTA (e.g. "Contact", "Book intro call").

---

## 3. Expertise — Hub (How We Help)

- **A. Positioning of Expertise**
  - 1–2 paragraphs summarizing how you think about GTM expertise across pillars.
  - Clarify that each pillar represents a set of related capabilities.

- **B. Overview of Pillars**
  - Short description of each pillar:
    - Content & Engagement
    - Demand & Growth
    - Strategy & Insights
    - Systems & Operations
  - 1–2 sentence summary for each pillar, focused on outcomes.

- **C. When to Engage (Symptoms / Triggers)**
  - Common pain points that signal a need for help.
  - Map pains loosely back to pillars (e.g. “low engagement” → Content & Engagement).

- **D. How Engagements Work**
  - Typical steps or phases (discover, design, implement, optimize).
  - Expected collaboration style with clients (hands-on, embedded, advisory).

- **E. Featured Pillars / Topics**
  - 2–4 highlighted pillars or topics that are most relevant right now.
  - Short blurbs + links to pillar pages and/or specific topics.

- **F. CTA to Explore Pillars**
  - Encourage visitors to browse topics relevant to their role / challenge.
  - Links to each pillar page.

---

## 4. Expertise — Pillar Page (e.g. Content & Engagement)

- **A. Pillar Introduction**
  - Brief definition of the pillar in the context of GTM.
  - Why this pillar matters for growth and GTM performance.

- **B. Outcomes & Value**
  - 3–5 specific business outcomes (e.g. “higher MQL to SQL conversion”).
  - Short explanation for each outcome.

- **C. Common Challenges**
  - 4–6 common pain points or failure modes this pillar addresses.

- **D. Capabilities / Services Within the Pillar**
  - Bulleted list of topics/services (these map to individual expertise topic pages).
  - 1–2 line description for each capability.

- **E. Example Engagements / Use Cases**
  - 2–4 short example scenarios where this pillar is applied.
  - Optional references to related case studies.

- **F. Cross-Pillar Connections**
  - Explain how this pillar intersects with the others (e.g. Content & Engagement + Demand & Growth).

- **G. CTA to Topics / Contact**
  - Prompt to explore individual topic pages.
  - Secondary CTA to start a conversation about this pillar.

---

## 5. Expertise — Individual Topic Page

*(For specific services such as "Account-Based Marketing (ABM)", "CRM Management & Integration", etc.)*

- **A. Topic Summary**
  - Clear definition of the topic/service.
  - 1–2 sentences on why it matters now.

- **B. Who This Is For**
  - Typical roles (e.g. VP Marketing, RevOps lead, founder).
  - Stage / company types where this service is most relevant.

- **C. Problems We Solve**
  - 4–6 concrete problems that this service addresses.

- **D. What We Do (Scope)**
  - List of typical activities / deliverables.
  - Clarify what is included and what is usually out of scope.

- **E. Approach / Method**
  - 3–5 step approach or framework.

- **F. Proof / Examples**
  - Short bullets tying this topic to case studies or outcomes.

- **G. Next Steps CTA**
  - Invite them to talk specifically about this topic.
  - Link to contact / booking and optionally related case studies.

---

## 6. Industries — Hub (Who We Help)

- **A. Positioning of Industries**
  - 1–2 paragraphs on the types of companies you serve.
  - Explain why industry context matters for GTM.

- **B. Industry List Overview**
  - Short grouped list of all industries (B2B SaaS, Healthcare, Financial Services, etc.).
  - 1-line descriptor or typical challenge for each group if helpful.

- **C. Horizontal Themes Across Industries**
  - 3–5 GTM themes that cut across industries (e.g. long sales cycles, complex buying committees).

- **D. Why Industry Experience Matters**
  - Explain how prior experience speeds execution and reduces risk.

- **E. CTA to Industry Pages**
  - Invite visitors to choose the industry most like them.
  - Grid or list linking to each individual industry page.

---

## 7. Industries — Individual Industry Page

*(For specific industries such as “B2B SaaS”, “Healthcare & Health Technology”, “Financial Services”, etc.)*

- **A. Industry Overview**
  - 1–2 paragraph summary of the industry context.
  - Key GTM constraints (e.g. regulations, procurement, complex stakeholders).

- **B. Ideal Customers Within This Industry**
  - Describe the types of companies you work best with (size, model, buyer types).

- **C. Common GTM Challenges**
  - 4–6 specific problems seen repeatedly in this industry.

- **D. How We Help This Industry**
  - Map your pillars or specific services to this industry (e.g. ABM for cybersecurity).

- **E. Representative Case Studies**
  - 2–4 brief references to relevant case studies or anonymized examples.

- **F. Engagement Models / Typical Projects**
  - Common project shapes or engagement types for this industry.

- **G. CTA Tailored to the Industry**
  - Clear next step framed in that industry’s language.

---

## 8. Case Studies — Hub

- **A. Introduction / Why Case Studies Matter**
  - Short framing of why you showcase work this way.

- **B. How to Browse**
  - Explain filters or categories (by pillar, by industry, by outcome).

- **C. Highlighted / Signature Case Studies**
  - 3–6 featured stories with a 1-line headline + key metric/outcome.

- **D. Case Study List / Grid**
  - Consistent preview pattern: client type, problem, outcome, pillar/industry tags.

- **E. CTA to Work Together**
  - Short prompt tying their situation back to the outcomes shown.

---

## 9. Case Study — Detail Page

- **A. Snapshot / Summary Block**
  - Client type (anonymized if needed), industry, size.
  - Problem statement in 1–2 sentences.
  - 2–4 headline outcomes (metrics where possible).

- **B. Context & Challenge**
  - Deeper background: what was broken or constrained.
  - Why it was urgent or important to solve.

- **C. Objectives**
  - 3–5 explicit objectives or success criteria agreed with the client.

- **D. Approach**
  - High-level phases or steps.
  - How collaboration worked (stakeholders, cadence, tools).

- **E. Solutions & Work Delivered**
  - Key initiatives or artifacts you delivered.
  - Tie each back to GTM pillars or topics where relevant.

- **F. Results**
  - Quantitative results where possible (lift, pipeline, velocity, efficiency).
  - Qualitative improvements (alignment, visibility, confidence).

- **G. Lessons & Takeaways**
  - 2–4 insights that a reader can apply to their own situation.

- **H. CTA / Related Links**
  - Prompt to discuss a similar challenge.
  - Links to related case studies, relevant expertise topics, or industry pages.

---

## 10. How to Use This Document

- **For writing**: Start with the correct page type above, write each section in A → B → C order, and keep each section’s purpose clear and focused.
- **For templates**: Developers can mirror these sections as components or slots in the corresponding page templates.
- **For review**: When content is “done”, verify that each required section exists, in order, even if some are intentionally very short.
